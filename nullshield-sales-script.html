// Before you make a single call or send a single email 1. Know their company. Spend 5 minutes on their website, LinkedIn, and recent news.
2. Know their industry's specific threat landscape. A fintech CISO cares about fraud APIs. A healthtech CTO fears HIPAA fines.
3. Lead with a problem they have — not a product you sell.
4. Never pitch on the first touch. Your goal is a 15-minute call, nothing more.
5. CISOs are trained to detect threats. Generic outreach registers as spam — or worse, a phishing test.

Cold Call Scripts by Persona

Target call window: Tuesday–Thursday, 7:30–9:00am or 4:30–5:30pm local time. Avoid Mondays and Fridays. Keep the first call under 3 minutes.
// Opening (first 10 seconds — critical)
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from NullShield. I'll be direct — this is a cold call. Do you have 90 seconds?"
// Why this worksAsking permission immediately disarms defensiveness. CISOs respect directness. "90 seconds" sounds painless and they almost always say yes.
// If they say yes — the hook (30 seconds)
"We work with CISOs at [similar company type, e.g., Series B fintechs] who are dealing with the same thing right now: their infrastructure is growing faster than their security posture can keep up, and the board is asking for answers they don't have. NullShield deploys threat monitoring and zero-trust controls in days, not months — without touching your existing stack. I'm not trying to sell you anything today. I'm trying to find out if it's even worth a 15-minute conversation. Is that a problem you're sitting on right now?"
// If they engage — qualifying questions (pick 1–2, don't interrogate)
"Just so I don't waste your time — what does your current monitoring setup look like?" "Are you under any compliance pressure right now? SOC 2, ISO, anything board-level?" "When a threat fires at 2am, who's actually waking up?" "When's the last time you did a full attack surface audit?"
// The close (booking a meeting)
"Here's what I'd suggest — let me send you a quick threat brief specific to [their industry, e.g., payments infrastructure]. No pitch, just intel. If it's useful, we can get 15 minutes on the calendar. What's your email?" --- OR if they're warmer --- "I'd love to show you what our deployment looks like against an environment similar to yours. I can do Tuesday at 10am or Thursday late afternoon — what works better?"
// If they say "not interested"
"Totally fair. Can I ask — is it timing, or is security coverage genuinely not a gap for you right now? I want to make sure I'm not calling back when it's still not relevant." [Listen carefully — their answer tells you whether to follow up in 90 days or remove them from your list.]

Cold Email Templates

Keep emails under 100 words. Subject lines under 6 words. Never use "I hope this email finds you well." One call to action maximum. Send Tuesday–Thursday, 6–8am or 4–6pm.

// Template 1 — The Trigger Email (best performing)

Use when: A company in their industry just had a breach. Send within 48 hours of news breaking.
To: [CISO/CTO Name] <[email]>
Subject: After the [Company X] breach
[Name], The [Company X] breach this week hit [industry, e.g., fintech payments infrastructure] specifically — the same attack surface your team is managing. The vector was [attack type, e.g., misconfigured API gateway with exposed credentials]. It's not exotic. It's the kind of gap NullShield closes in a first deployment. Worth 15 minutes to see if you're exposed to the same thing? [Your Name] NullShield

// Template 2 — The Free Scan Offer

Use when: Targeting CTOs at Series A/B startups. Works especially well for companies that just raised funding.
To: [CTO Name] <[email]>
Subject: Free scan of [company].com
[Name], Saw [Company] just closed your [funding round]. Congrats — now your attack surface just became a lot more interesting to adversaries. We run passive external attack surface scans for companies at your stage — no credentials, nothing invasive. You get a report of what's visible from the outside: open ports, exposed endpoints, leaked credentials in breach databases. We'll do it for free. Takes us 24 hours. Want us to kick one off? [Your Name] NullShield

// Template 3 — Compliance Angle (GRC/CISO)

Use when: Company is approaching SOC 2, ISO, or HIPAA certification. Look for compliance-related job postings as a trigger.
To: [CISO/GRC Manager] <[email]>
Subject: SOC 2 audit prep — one thing most teams miss
[Name], Most companies going through SOC 2 for the first time spend 3–6 months manually gathering evidence that should have been collected automatically the whole time. NullShield generates continuous audit evidence — logs, control mappings, risk documentation — so your next audit is a download, not a fire drill. // Template 4 — The Direct Ask (for warm-ish contacts)

To: [Name] <[email]>
Subject: Quick question about your security coverage
[Name], One question: when a threat fires in your environment at 2am, does someone wake up? If the answer is "we're not sure" or "it depends" — that's the gap NullShield closes. 24/7 SOC coverage and automated response, deployed in days. 15 minutes this week? I'll send a calendar link if so. [Your Name] NullShield

// Follow-Up Email (Day 5 if no reply)

Subject: Re: [original subject]
[Name], Bumping this in case it got buried. I'll make it simple: if protecting [company]'s infrastructure isn't a priority right now, just say the word and I'll leave you alone. If it is — 15 minutes is all I need. [Your Name]

Subject: Closing the loop

Either way, I'll stop reaching out after this. If security coverage ever becomes a conversation at [Company], I'd love to be in the room. [Your Name] NullShield P.S. The offer for a free external scan stands indefinitely — [your email] whenever you want it.

LinkedIn DM Scripts

LinkedIn is for awareness and warmth, not for closing. Keep messages under 50 words. Never paste your email pitch into LinkedIn — it reads as lazy. Comment on their content before DMing.

// Connection Request Note (300 char limit)

// Generic (when you have no specific hook)
"[Name] — I work with CISOs at [their industry] companies on threat visibility and zero-trust. Not pitching — just building a network of security leaders. Happy to share intel if ever useful. — [Your Name], NullShield"

"[Name] — your post on [topic they posted about] was spot on. We're seeing the same pattern with [specific detail related to their post]. Running into anything similar on your end? Would love to swap notes."

Objection Handling

"We already have a security solution."
"That's great — most of our clients do too. We're not a replacement, we layer on top of what you have. The gap we usually fill is 24/7 SOC coverage and automated response — the stuff that's hard to staff in-house. What does your current incident response look like after hours?"
"We don't have budget right now."
"I get it. Let me ask this: what's the cost if you had a breach in the next 90 days? The average is $4.5M. I'm not saying that will happen — but I want to make sure budget conversations are happening with the full picture. Can we do a 15-minute call so you have the information if the conversation comes up?"
"Send me some information."
"Absolutely. Rather than send generic docs, let me tailor it to your environment. Two quick questions: are you more concerned about external threats or internal/insider risk right now? And are you under any compliance requirements? I'll send you exactly what's relevant."
"We handle security in-house."
"Good teams do — and they're usually the ones we work with best, because they know exactly what they don't have bandwidth for. The question isn't whether your team is capable, it's whether you want them spending cycles on 2am alert triage or on strategic security architecture. Which is the bigger issue for you right now?"
"We're not a target — we're too small."
"I hear this a lot, and it made sense five years ago. Today, 43% of cyberattacks target small businesses specifically — because they're easier. Attackers don't choose targets by prestige; they choose by vulnerability. You're not too small to be profitable to a ransomware gang. Can I send you what we've seen in your industry this quarter?"
"We're in a vendor freeze / too busy right now."
"Totally understand — timing matters. When does your next planning cycle kick off? I'd rather get on your radar now so that when you're ready to evaluate, we're already in the conversation. Can I follow up in [specific month]?"

[Set a specific date, put it in your CRM, and actually follow up.]
"How are you different from CrowdStrike / Palo Alto?"
"Great question — and I'll be honest: they have great endpoint products. The gap they leave is full-stack coverage, human SOC response, and deployment that doesn't require a 6-month integration project. We're deployed in days, not quarters, and you get a human analyst on the phone when something fires — not a ticketing queue. What does your current deployment timeline look like?"
"I need to involve [other stakeholder] first."
"Absolutely — this kind of decision should involve multiple stakeholders. Can I ask who else would be in that conversation? I'd love to make sure I'm sending you the right materials for each person. And would it make sense to include [them] on a single intro call rather than going back and forth?"

Trigger Events — When to Strike

// The 48-hour rule Trigger-based outreach sent within 48 hours of an event gets 8–15x higher response rates than generic cold outreach. Build a monitoring system. Speed is your edge.
// Trigger: Company just raised funding
Use for Series A / B CTOs and CISOs "[Name], congrats on the [round]. At this stage, your attack surface just tripled — new infra, new team, new compliance scrutiny from investors. Most companies at your stage are 6 months from needing SOC 2 and 12 months from a board asking hard questions about security posture. We've helped [3–4 companies at similar stage] get ahead of it before it became urgent. 15 minutes?"
// Trigger: New CISO was just hired
Use for The newly hired CISO — highest conversion trigger that exists "[Name], saw you just joined [Company] as CISO. The first 90 days are usually a vendor evaluation sprint — everyone pitching you at once. I'll be different: I'd rather give you something useful before asking for anything. Happy to run a free external attack surface scan on your new environment — just so you know what you're walking into. Interested?"
// Trigger: Industry peer breach in the news
Time-sensitive — send within 24hrs "[Name], the [Company X] breach this week is the third [industry] incident this quarter using the same attack vector: [describe in plain language]. Your infrastructure runs on [similar tech if known], which has the same exposure. I don't want to be alarmist — I want to show you exactly where you stand. Can we do 15 minutes this week?"
// Trigger: Job posting for security role
Signal: they're aware of the gap "[Name], noticed [Company] is hiring a [Security Engineer / CISO / etc.]. That usually means one of two things: you're scaling security fast, or you've got a gap that needs filling now. While you're searching, NullShield can plug in immediately — no hiring cycle, no onboarding lag. Worth a quick call to see if we can bridge the gap while you build out the team?"
// Trigger: Compliance deadline approaching (SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO)
Find by: companies posting compliance engineer roles or audit prep content "[Name], companies going through SOC 2 for the first time typically underestimate the evidence collection by about 3 months. NullShield automates 80% of the evidence gathering — log collection, control documentation, access reviews — continuously, so there's nothing to scramble for when auditors show up. When's your next audit window? Happy to walk you through what we cover."
